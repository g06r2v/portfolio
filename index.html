Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between SIP and Lumpsum?

+

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) involves regular monthly investments, while lumpsum is a one-time investment. SIP helps average out market volatility through rupee cost averaging.

What are ELSS funds and their tax benefits?

+

ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme) mutual funds offer tax deduction up to ₹1.5 lakh under Section 80C with a 3-year lock-in period.

What documents are required for investment?

+

PAN card, Aadhaar card, bank account proof, and recent photograph are required for KYC compliance as per SEBI regulations.

What is NISM-Series-5A certification?

+

NISM-Series-5A is a professional certification for mutual fund distributors, ensuring compliance with regulatory standards and professional competency.

How do you assess risk profile?

+

Risk profiling assesses your financial goals, time horizon, income stability, and risk appetite to recommend suitable investment options.

What should I know before investing?

+

As per SEBI guidelines, investors must read all scheme-related documents carefully before investing to understand risks, charges, and investment objectives.
